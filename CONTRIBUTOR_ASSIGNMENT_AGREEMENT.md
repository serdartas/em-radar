# EM Radar Contributor Assignment Agreement

This Contributor Assignment Agreement ("Agreement") documents the rights granted by a person
or legal entity ("You") contributing to EM Radar to **Serdar Tas** ("Assignee"). It is a
legally binding agreement. Review it carefully and obtain independent legal advice if needed.

You accept this Agreement by submitting a contribution with the acceptance statement required
by [`CONTRIBUTING.md`](CONTRIBUTING.md). This Agreement applies to every contribution You
submit on or after the date You first accept it.

## 1. Definitions

**Contribution** means any original work of authorship, including source code, documentation,
configuration, designs, tests, or other material, that You intentionally submit to the EM
Radar repository for inclusion in the project. It does not include material that You
conspicuously mark in writing as "Not a Contribution."

**Submit** means any electronic, written, or verbal communication sent to the Assignee or the
project's representatives for the purpose of discussing or improving EM Radar, including pull
requests, commits, issues, and discussions.

## 2. Copyright Assignment

To the maximum extent permitted by applicable law, You hereby irrevocably assign to the
Assignee all worldwide right, title, and interest in and to the copyright, neighboring rights,
and database rights covering each Contribution, including all rights to reproduce, modify,
prepare derivative works, publicly display, publicly perform, sublicense, distribute, and
otherwise exploit the Contribution.

To the extent any such rights cannot legally be assigned, You grant the Assignee an
irrevocable, perpetual, worldwide, exclusive, transferable, fully paid-up, royalty-free
license, with the right to sublicense through multiple tiers, to exercise all such rights.

The Assignee grants You a perpetual, worldwide, non-exclusive, royalty-free, irrevocable
license to use, reproduce, modify, distribute, and otherwise exploit Your original
Contribution, including under other licenses, provided that You do not represent that the
resulting work is endorsed by EM Radar or the Assignee.

## 3. Moral Rights

To the maximum extent permitted by applicable law, You waive and agree not to assert any
moral rights, rights of attribution, rights of integrity, or similar rights that You may have
in a Contribution. Where such rights cannot be waived, You consent to the Assignee exercising
the assigned or licensed rights without restriction by those rights.

## 4. Patent License

You grant the Assignee and recipients of software distributed by the Assignee a perpetual,
worldwide, non-exclusive, transferable, royalty-free, irrevocable patent license to make,
have made, use, offer to sell, sell, import, and otherwise transfer each Contribution, where
that license applies only to patent claims that You can license and that are necessarily
infringed by the Contribution alone or by its combination with EM Radar.

## 5. Your Representations

You represent that:

- You are legally entitled to grant the rights in this Agreement.
- Each Contribution is Your original creation, except for third-party material that You
  clearly identify and that is permitted for inclusion in EM Radar.
- If Your employer or another party has rights in a Contribution, You have obtained
  authorization to make the Contribution and enter into this Agreement on that party's
  behalf, or that party has separately accepted this Agreement.
- To Your knowledge, the Contribution does not violate any third party's intellectual
  property, privacy, or other rights.
- You will notify the Assignee if any statement in this section becomes inaccurate.

## 6. Project Licensing and No Obligation

The Assignee may license, relicense, or otherwise use a Contribution on any terms, including
open-source and commercial terms. Contributions included in the public EM Radar repository
remain available under the repository's applicable open-source license.

The Assignee is not obligated to use, merge, or distribute any Contribution. You are not
expected to provide support for a Contribution unless You separately agree to do so.

## 7. Electronic Acceptance

You agree that electronic acceptance of this Agreement is intended to have the same legal
effect as a handwritten signature. The GitHub account submitting the acceptance statement,
the associated pull request or Contribution, and GitHub's records may be used as evidence of
acceptance.

If You submit on behalf of a legal entity, Your acceptance must identify the entity and You
represent that You have authority to bind it. The Assignee may require a separately signed
agreement before accepting any Contribution.

## 8. Entire Agreement

This Agreement is the entire agreement between You and the Assignee concerning its subject
matter and supersedes prior statements concerning rights in Contributions covered by it. Any
amendment or waiver must be in writing and accepted by both You and the Assignee. If any
provision is unenforceable, the remaining provisions remain in effect.

This Agreement is based in part on the copyright-assignment approach described by the
[Harmony Agreements](https://www.harmonyagreements.org/).
